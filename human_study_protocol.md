# Proposed Human-Subject Pilot (Not Yet Conducted)

This document is a study protocol, not evidence that participants were recruited
or that ethics approval was obtained. Data collection must not begin until the
authors obtain the approval or exemption required by their institution.

## Design

- Within-subject comparison of Softmax Utility AI, MarkovBelief, plain QLBS,
  QLBS with anchoring, and QLBS with periodic re-randomization.
- Target sample: 36 consenting adults, with the final sample size fixed before
  inspecting outcomes.
- Counterbalance controller order with a Latin square and randomize scenario order.
- Primary task: predict the NPC's next action after a short interaction history.
- Secondary ratings: believability, repetitiveness, responsiveness, and enjoyment
  on pre-specified seven-point Likert scales.

## Analysis plan

- Primary endpoint: within-participant next-action prediction accuracy.
- Compare controllers with a Friedman test; use paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests
  for planned post-hoc contrasts with Holm correction.
- Report effect sizes, confidence intervals, exclusions, missing observations,
  and all tested outcomes.
- Analyze presentation-order effects and retain them as a covariate/sensitivity
  check rather than silently pooling them.

## Ethics and data handling

- Obtain written informed consent and institutional approval/exemption first.
- Collect no names in the analysis dataset; assign random participant IDs.
- Store consent records separately from responses.
- Publish only de-identified data and the final questionnaire, subject to the
  approved consent language.
